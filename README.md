# Breaking History! Chongqing University Multicopter Team Wins National First Prize

From October 5th to 13th, 2025, the China University Aircraft Design Innovation Competition, hosted by the Chinese Society of Aeronautics and Astronautics, was held in Deqing, Zhejiang. The competition attracted faculty and students from top universities including Tsinghua University, Beihang University, Harbin Institute of Technology, Northwestern Polytechnical University, and Chongqing University, making the competition exceptionally fierce.

![Introduction](https://github.com/LANEING-AVIATION/CUADC/blob/main/IMG1.png)

In this high-level contest, the Chongqing University multi-rotor project team achieved a **historical breakthrough**, winning the **National First Prize** and the **Team Second Prize**, setting a new all-time best record for the university in this competition.

![Introduction](https://github.com/LANEING-AVIATION/CUADC/blob/main/IMG2.png)

The participating team was led and advised by teachers Liu Hao and Wei Zhen. The team members included eight outstanding students: Kong Pincheng, Ma Ziheng, Zhang Wenhao, Jin Meihan, Cui Rongyu, Zhuo Junxiong, Ding Da, and Yang Zhuofei.

![Introduction](https://github.com/LANEING-AVIATION/CUADC/blob/main/IMG3.png)

### Project Highlights: Leading Edge Technology, Multi-Disciplinary Collaboration

The success of this project team was not accidental. Relying on the high-level platform of the Chongqing University Student Cross-Innovation Center, they dedicated themselves to the transition from traditional model aircraft to **flying robots**, actively introducing cutting-edge technologies such as **edge computing**, **machine learning**, and **robot operating systems**. The team implemented efficient **Product Lifecycle Management** (PLM), achieving online hosting of mechanical design, code repositories, and container images. They correctly handled the relationship between design and manufacturing, and experimentation and simulation, adhering to the scientific method of **“Simulation First, Experimental Verification”**.

![Introduction](https://github.com/LANEING-AVIATION/CUADC/blob/main/IMG4.png)

The multi-rotor team is a typical **multi-disciplinary collaboration team**. Their members come from different majors, integrating cutting-edge knowledge from fields such as **mechanical engineering**, **electrical engineering**, **communication**, and **computer vision**:

* **Refined Algorithms Foundation:** Kong Pincheng and Cui Rongyu were mainly responsible for flight control program development and closed-loop control, developing reliable flight control algorithms.
  
![Introduction](https://github.com/LANEING-AVIATION/CUADC/blob/main/IMG5.png)

* **High-Reliability Mechanical Structure:** Ma Ziheng and Jin Meihan pioneered the high-reliability mechanical structure design, particularly the **stable, precise, and lightweight landing gear**, which stood out in the competition.

![Introduction](https://github.com/LANEING-AVIATION/CUADC/blob/main/IMG6.png)

* **Structure Optimization and Layout:** Zhuo Junxiong continuously improved the structural design and optimized the equipment layout during assembly, significantly enhancing flight performance.
* **Advanced Communication:** Zhang Wenhao introduced an advanced **global Ethernet communication system** to the aircraft, building a powerful "nerve center".
* **Strong Environmental Adaptability:** Ding Da demonstrated exceptional adaptability, training the visual model in real-time based on changes in the competition environment, enabling the aircraft to achieve the strongest adaptability.

![Introduction](https://github.com/LANEING-AVIATION/CUADC/blob/main/IMG7.png)

**On-Site Adaptation and Improvement:** Team members were able to improve the aircraft and train models on-site according to the specific competition environment, demonstrating extremely high engineering practical ability and team resilience.

![Introduction](https://github.com/LANEING-AVIATION/CUADC/blob/main/IMG8.png)
